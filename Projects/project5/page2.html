Push-Ups

The push up is a great compound exercise that strengthens your upper body. To perform, turn your hands outwards, retract your scapula, go down, and push up.

Pull-Ups

The pull up is also a great compound exercise that strengthens your upper body. Just hanging onto the bar has a lot of benefits. To perform, simply retract your scapula, grip the bar and pull up.

Cardiovascular Exercises

Your cardiovascular health is crucial because it keeps your heart and lungs healthy. It is recommended that you get in at least 5000 steps per day. That is approximately 2 miles.

Squats

The squat is the most versatile leg exercise because it targets basically every muscle of the lowerbody. To perform, arch your hips back, lower yourself, and stand up. Adjust weight as needed.

Leg-Raises

Leg-raises are great for working the muscles in your abdomen. To perform, lay down and raise your legs up and down slowly. The more you straighten your legs, the harder this exercise gets.

Key Take-Aways:

There you have it! The exercises I included above require essentially no equipment and stregnthens all the muscles throughout your body. Consistency is key and these exercises will make your body a lot more functional and healthy.
